About Us

The Flat Earth Society is a group actively promoting the Flat Earth Movement worldwide. Descending from Samuel Shenton's International Flat Earth Research Society, and the universal Zetetic Society before it, we continue the age-old tradition of questioning the Round Earth doctrine and challenging authorities.

The Society's Mission

Samuel Shenton, a Fellow of the Royal Astronomical Society and the Royal Geographic Society, founded the Flat Earth Society in 1956, after renaming it from the Universal Zetetic Society.

He led the Flat Earth Society from its founding in 1956 until his death in 1971, at which time leadership passed to Charles K. Johnson. Shenton collected material, gave lectures and generated publicity for the Society so that by his death in 1971 the Society had over 100 members.

Flat Earth beliefs have had a notable recent resurgence since 2015. Since 2017, there are now over 500 members.

Drawing from the society's founder, Samuel Shenton, our goal is to foster a community where we can discuss and challenge the mainstream scientific understanding the the Earth's shape.
